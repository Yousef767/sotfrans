Transcreation

Transcreation is taking the essence of a message and re-creating it in another language or dialect, considering the cultural aspects of the target audience. It is a transformation of an overall message which tackles not only written content but also visual design and imagery aspects, taking into consideration the cultural context and keeping the message main purpose, tone and spirit. Transcreation is a relatively new term which was coined by the advertising, entertainment and language industries, and has become more well-known only in the past decade. Its origin clearly shows why it is mainly used with the translation of marketing and advertising material specifically.

As its name suggests, transcreation is a translation process carried out in a certain creative way to make a message meaningful to a particular audience. This process requires a 360 degree overview at a message and adapting it to the target audience and culture, while keeping the main design elements in place in order to maintain brand consistency between national markets.

Translation vs. Transcreation

Translation focuses on taking your content as written in one language, and changing it into another without any change applied to the overall structure and presentation of your content. In transcreation however, there is much greater liberty with the structure of the original content that may be modified provided that this wouldnâ€™t alter the basic message of the content. Transcreation focuses less on sentence structure and sentence by sentence accuracy, and more on conveying the message without relying on the original structure, tone and flow of the original. We recreate the text, including all puns, jargon, connotations and references, layout, images, and coloring, but from the perspective of the target market.
